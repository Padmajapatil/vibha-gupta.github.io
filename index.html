My Story

I am a former public health expert and brand strategist who believes in the power of communication for good. I have led and contributed to strategy that has helped brands grow, solved crisis situations, enter new markets and reboot in existing markets.

Over past 16+years I have worked in advertising, built two start-ups, earned a PhD and turned into a conscious urban farmer. I have worked on brands like Coca-Cola, Dettol, Mastercard, Nestle, Wrigley’s and Dabur (to name a few). I have also worked on large scale public health initiatives like Banega Swachh India (Reckitt), Malnutrition (Unicef), Breastfeeding promotion (Ministry of Women & Child Development, Nestle) among others. Over the years, my work has been recognized for strategic effectiveness at national, APAC and Global stages including the Effies, Appies, Jay Chiat, AMEs and WARC Effectiveness Awards.

I have been a visiting faculty with several B-schools as well as graduate and post-graduate courses with Delhi University. I continue to participate in both National and International conferences as an academic researcher and an Industry Professional. I have also published several research papers and industry case studies during my career. Distilling the various learnings along the way, I now have created a proprietary framework that puts curiosity at the center. It asks questions around our memory structures, decodes the social norms, delves into the semiotics and existing vocabulary construct, draws on visual culture while mapping consumer journey to inform the solution narrative.
